Spaghetti Bolognese

Food Description

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian-inspired dish known for its hearty and flavorful meat sauce, served over a bed of al dente spaghetti. The sauce, originating from Bologna, Italy, typically combines ground beef or pork with onions, garlic, tomatoes, and a blend of herbs like basil and oregano, simmered together to develop a rich, savory taste. This dish is beloved worldwide for its comforting, home-cooked appeal and is often garnished with grated Parmesan cheese to add a touch of sharpness and depth. It's a versatile meal that can be easily adjusted to suit different tastes and preferences, making it a staple in many households.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 400g ground beef
  • 400g can of crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, to serve

Steps

  1. In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. While the spaghetti cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until softened and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the ground beef to the skillet. Cook, breaking the meat apart with a spoon, until it’s no longer pink, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, and basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the sauce simmer over low heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Once the sauce has thickened, taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Toss the cooked spaghetti in the sauce until evenly coated. Serve hot, sprinkled with grated Parmesan cheese.

This straightforward recipe will give you a delicious Spaghetti Bolognese that feels like it’s straight from an Italian home kitchen. Buon appetito!
