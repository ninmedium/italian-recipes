Lasagna

Food Description

Lasagna is a wide, flat sheet of pasta. Lasagna can refer to either the type of noodle or to the typical lasagna dish which is a dish made with several layers of lasagna sheets with sauce and other ingredients, such as meats and cheese, in between the lasagna noodles.

Ingredients

  • lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 jar (24 ounces) of your favorite marinara sauce
  • 1 container (15 ounces) ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside. Add a little oil to the water to prevent sticking.
  3. In a skillet over medium heat, brown the ground beef until no longer pink. Season with a little salt and pepper. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Stir the marinara sauce into the cooked meat and simmer for about 5 minutes. This is your meat sauce.
  5. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese, 1 cup of mozzarella cheese, the Parmesan cheese, and the egg. This is your cheese mixture.
  6. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, start with a layer of meat sauce, then a layer of noodles (3 should fit across), and a layer of the cheese mixture. Repeat the layers, ending with meat sauce on top.
  7. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil, sprinkle the remaining mozzarella cheese on top, and bake uncovered for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  8. Let the lasagna cool for about 10 minutes before slicing. Garnish with chopped parsley if desired, and serve warm.

Enjoy your meal! This simple recipe brings a taste of Italy right into your home with minimal effort.
